NetArt Presentation

Making fun of the netart archive page

Scaling differences changing effective width of the display medium

Two displays were used, both at 1920x1080. The difference is that one has the size of apps and text on the main display scaled to 100%, and the other to 125%. These are the default values for a desktop and a laptop computer. This translates to a large screen and a small screen (medium?) The most immediately obvious difference is that the display with larger text obviously could not fit as many items as the small display.

These images were captured on two different machines that have different physical dimensions. A similar effect could technically be produced by simply scaling the browser window, but would result in lower resolution screenshots.

Text size

On the small screen, the text appears with approximately similar physical size, but is scaled up 125% relative to the size of the screen. The text stretches across the entire page with much less margin. Despite having less side margin, there are still more lines indicating that the margins were not enough to compensate for reduced screen width.

Overlap

Due to having much smaller margin on the text, the net art anthology header at the top left occludes text on the smaller display. Box items later appear over the header item though.

Gallery width

The smaller screen was only able to fit two items per row in it's display galleries, compared to three items per row in the larger screen. This goes down to one with a narrower display, and presumably would continue scaling up with a wider display.

Awkward last item

There were an odd number of items in this display, and a tall item second to last. When displaying on a n by 2 grid, this results in a hanging box that's separated from it's upper neighbor.

Edge justification

On the larger screen, there is enough space between the centered display and the edge of the screen that an edge-justified box fits within that margin.

End of document

The gallery at the end also had a different number of suggested works per row.

Proposed solution

So I've mostly just been making fun of how the grid alignment looks funky when scaled to a different size. The simplest solution in this case would be to center the gallery, so that things don't appear off to the side.
